Important Safety Notes

Stop immediately if you experience sharp pain, numbness, or tingling. These stretches should create a gentle pulling sensation, not pain. Since you're experiencing knee pain with weight-bearing, be extra cautious with any movements that stress the knee joint.

üéØ Rowing-Specific Recovery Tips

  • Rowing without foot straps: This modification you mentioned is excellent! It reduces hip flexor engagement and forces better posture. Start with very short, easy sessions.
  • Focus on the drive phase: When you return to normal rowing, concentrate on pushing through your heels and engaging glutes rather than pulling with hip flexors.
  • Seat height adjustment: Sometimes lowering the seat slightly can reduce hip flexion at the catch.
  • Pre-rowing warm-up: Always do the hip flexor stretch and clamshells before rowing sessions.
  • Ice after activity: If you experience any inflammation, ice for 15-20 minutes post-exercise.
  • Monitor symptoms: Track pain levels daily. If knee pain persists or worsens, consider seeing a sports medicine physician.
